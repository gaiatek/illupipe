Preprocessing

Before performing any gene expression comparison, various manipulations are needed to reduce sources of variation. First, intensities are adjusted by replacing any value below background by the average background value over all arrays. Then, all intensities are log2 transformed before being normalized. Filtering allows to remove genes that are unlikely to carry information about the phenotypes of interest due to their low overall intensity or variability. This filtering step will improve the selection of differentially expressed genes with high statistical significance. Finally, technical replicates may be averaged to yield the expression values used for the subsequent analysis.
