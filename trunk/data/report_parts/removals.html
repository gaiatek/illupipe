Quality Control, Array Removal
Quality assessment is achieved as a preliminary step to any microarray analysis. Beforehand, the scanned array images are visually inspected for artifacts or mishandling. Illumina beadArrays provide control probes used to compare arrays within a chip. Diagnostic plots such as density plot, box plot and a heatmap of between-array distances are used to assess hybridization quality within the whole data set. Abnormal arrays can be easily identified by looking at these diagnostic plots.
Note that a given sample can be absent from the final analysis. This is either because it never made it to hybridization (e.g. bad RNA quality or not hybridized yet) or as a result of array removal by the data analyst.
