Gene Set Enrichment Analysis
Pathway analysis methods focus on the behavior of a gene set rather than individual genes by evaluating gene expression profiles of a-priori defined biological pathways in association with a phenotype of interest. We will use Gene Set Enrichment Analysis (GSEA ) a non-parametric annotation-driven statistical analysis method. The basic idea of GSEA is to compare two phenotypes to determine whether a known biological pathway or sets of individual genes are significantly enriched in a list of genes ranked according to criteria such as the expression difference between the two phenotypes of interest. 

We will use and test systematically three Categories of gene sets from the Molecular signature Database (MsigDB, http://www.broad.mit.edu/gsea/msigdb) version 3.0 (Oct. 2010) composed of:
1)    880 gene sets (referenced as C2) collected from various sources such as online pathway databases, publications in PubMed, and knowledge of domain experts
2)   615 gene sets (referenced as C3) that contain genes that share a cis-regulatory motif that is conserved across the human, mouse, rat, and dog genomes and         represent known or predicted regulatory elements in promoters and 3'-UTRs matching transcription factor targets.
3)   825 gene sets of Biological Processes (referenced as C5) coming from the Gene Ontology Project.

Merged to C2 are 28 gene sets collected from transcriptional analyses of the blood in immunological context, and are named M x.x (eg. M 1.3). Their descriptions are not available through MsigDB, but can be found in Chaussabel et al. (2008) :
A Modular Analysis Framework for Blood Genomics Studies: Application to Systemic Lupus Erythematosus
Immunity, Volume 29, Issue 1, Pages 150-164

GSEA separate significantly regulated pathways between upregulated and downregulated pathways. For each contrast and each database, you can browse the regulated pathways sorted by decreasing significance. We also provide a Summary Figure, which represent the expression levels accross samples relevant to the contrast for the top 5 most significant genes from the top 5 up and the top 5 downregulated pathways. The pathway membership for all genes is represented in the left panel of the figure.
